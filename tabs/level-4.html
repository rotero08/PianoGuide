Level 4: Inversions, Speed & Polyrhythm

Your goal for this level

Read dynamics, articulation, ledger lines, and changing tempos on a first pass, read chord inversions by feel, and handle passages too fast for plain repetition along with your first three-against-four polyrhythm.

Reading becomes expressive and dense. Technique gains two specific tools for passages that resist the fingers, and you meet your first independent-rhythm problem.

Technique the hands · hands-separate

Two hands-separate tools for speed, plus your first independent-rhythm coordination.

    Bernhard's Chord Attack loopSpeed fix: block, then dotted rhythms.

    A well-known routine from the Piano Street forums for runs your fingers cannot yet play at speed. You squash the run into a single chord so the hand learns the shape, then practise it in dotted rhythms, which trains the finger transitions to fire quickly.

    Practice plan
    Apply to any stuck run. Done when the run plays at tempo, relaxed.
    Open resource ↗
    CrownStarr's 3-against-4 methodYour first real polyrhythm method.

    A clear community explanation of the first hard polyrhythm. Rather than counting frantically, you set a slow pulse on the main beat, practise each hand alone, then combine on two rules: keep each hand smooth and land together on the downbeat.

    Practice plan
    Slow macro pulse, hands separate then together. Done when four bars land together on each downbeat.
    Open resource ↗
    Inversions by feelRead chord shapes, not three letters.

    Reading E-G-C as C major in first inversion instead of three separate letters is a shape-recognition skill, and those shapes are drilled in the Keyboard Geometry tab. Knowing them by feel is what lets you read a full chord at a glance.

    Practice plan
    Drill in the Geometry tab daily. Done when any inversion is instant.
How the two halves connect hereWhen your reading outruns your hands, you switch tools. You read the expressive Mayron Cole and Gradus material hands-together for flow, but the instant a run resists, you pull it out and apply the Chord Attack loop hands-separate until it is relaxed at tempo, then fold it back in. Checkpoint

Checkpoint, am I ready for the next level?

≈ ABRSM / RCM Grade 4 (reading)

Confirm expressive reading and your first speed and independence tools.

  1. Sight-read an expressive piece with dynamics and ledger lines on the first pass.
  2. Read any triad inversion as a shape, instantly.
  3. Take a previously stuck run to target tempo with the Chord Attack loop, relaxed.
  4. Play four bars of three-against-four evenly, landing together on each downbeat.

When all four hold, move to Level 5, where the voices become fully independent.

Optional branch unlocked

Modern / Jazz Harmony is now available. With inversions reliable and chord reading solid, this is where extended chords (sevenths, ninths) and jazz lead sheets become approachable. Optional, and aimed at players who want pop, rock, or jazz alongside the classical spine.
