Technique the hands · hands-separate

Deepen your hand independence and add a relaxed octave technique.

    Kunz: 200 Canons (advanced numbers)Independence at higher tempos.

    Returning to the Kunz canons at higher numbers and faster tempos. The delayed imitation forces each hand to run its own motor program, the most direct way to push independence past the intermediate plateau.

    Practice plan
    Ten canons at tempo. Done when there is no timing lag.
    Open resource ↗
    Parallel Octave Speed-HingeRelaxed fast-octave technique.

    A community technique for fast octaves. You lock the hand into a stable octave frame but keep the wrist loose as a bouncing hinge, letting gravity strike each octave while the wrist releases. This prevents the fatigue that stiff octaves cause.

    Practice plan
    Apply to octave passages. Done when octaves stay even with no forearm burn.

How the two halves connect herePhrasing is itself a technical act. Shaping a Bach or Chopin line musically on sight requires the hand to be free, which is exactly what the octave-hinge and canon work provide. You read for phrasing hands-together, and any octave passage that tightens up gets the hinge treatment hands-separate.

Checkpoint, am I ready for the next level?

≈ ABRSM / RCM Grade 6 (reading)

Confirm phrasing-level reading and relaxed octaves.

  1. Sight-read a Bach invention with phrasing and ornaments intact.
  2. Play moving octaves evenly without forearm fatigue.
  3. Play ten Kunz canons hands-together at tempo with no timing lag.
  4. Run the four-part self-audit at Grade 6.

When all four hold, move to Level 7, the open-ended final stage.
