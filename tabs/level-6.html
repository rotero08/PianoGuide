How the two halves connect herePhrasing is itself a technical act. Shaping a Bach or Chopin line musically on sight requires the hand to be free, which is exactly what the octave-hinge and canon work provide. You read for phrasing hands-together, and any octave passage that tightens up gets the hinge treatment hands-separate.

Checkpoint

Checkpoint, am I ready for the next level?

≈ ABRSM / RCM Grade 6 (reading)

Confirm phrasing-level reading and relaxed octaves.

  1. Sight-read a Bach invention with phrasing and ornaments intact.
  2. Play moving octaves evenly without forearm fatigue.
  3. Play ten Kunz canons hands-together at tempo with no timing lag.
  4. Run the four-part self-audit at Grade 6.

When all four hold, move to Level 7, the open-ended final stage.

Level 6 benchmarks

How to use these benchmarks

A benchmark is a short, real piece, not an exercise. Its only job is to prove that the reading you built this level actually transfers to music before you move up. Do not drill it like repertoire.

  1. Listen to the recording once so you know the target sound.
  2. Sight-read the score cold, hands together, slow but steady — no stopping to fix notes.
  3. Pass: you get through it on the first or second attempt with a steady pulse and without watching your hands.
  4. Stall: if three honest attempts still fall apart, you are not ready. Don’t grind it — drop back, read easier material, then return. (This is the chart’s “New Game +”.)

The five era pieces (Baroque through Modern) test the same reading across different musical styles, so passing all five means your reading is general rather than pattern-matched to one composer. The free Mayron Cole Audition Book page is the quick pass/fail gate. Passing this level lines up roughly with around ABRSM / RCM Grade 5–6.
