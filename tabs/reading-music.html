How to Read Music

Before you practice sight-reading at the keys, ensure you understand the basic logic of notation. Our goal is to learn notation rules away from the keyboard first, so you don't have to think about them during practice.

Why we decode away from the keys: Sweller (1988)

Two staves, two clefs, ten fingers and a pedal blow past working memory's 4–7 item limit. Calculating individual pitch letters while hitting motor targets is a classic split-attention overload [5], and that overload shows up physically as locked wrists and shoulders. Learning notation rules first, and reading by interval rather than letter, keeps load under the tension threshold.

Read ahead, not at the cursor: Goebl & Palmer (2008)

Skilled readers sweep their eyes 1–2 beats ahead of their hands (saccades), pre-loading the next coordinates so touch can guide exact placement [8]. Falling-note apps force the eyes into "cursor lock," suppressing those forward saccades and blocking real spatial mapping. Always read relative vertical distances on a static grand staff.
