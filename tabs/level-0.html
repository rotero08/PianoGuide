Level 0: Standard Linear Reading

Your goal for this level

Read single notes on the grand staff with both hands together in a fixed five-finger (C) position, recognising intervals by their shape rather than spelling out every letter, while staying physically relaxed.

The five-line staff arrives. To keep the mental load low, your hands stay locked in one position, so almost all of your attention can go to the new job of tracking two staves at once.

Sight-Reading the eyes · hands-together

Stationary five-finger reading with no shifts and no leaps, just two clefs moving together.

    Kravchuk: 354 Reading Exercises in CFreeThe free fixed-position reading catalog.

    Michael Kravchuk, a performance graduate who publishes his books free as PDFs, wrote these specifically to fix the most common beginner flaw of neglecting the left hand. Every six-bar exercise keeps both hands in a fixed C position, so by removing all shifts and leaps it frees your whole attention to learn the one new skill of reading two clefs together.

Technique the hands · hands-separate

Adopt the relaxation method you will use for the rest of your playing life, and learn to decode notation away from the keys so it never competes with the hands.

    Chuan C. Chang: Fundamentals of Piano PracticeFreeThe free book behind hands-separate technique.

    The free book that the technique side of this guide is built on. Chang, a research scientist and pianist, argues that technical walls come from inefficient, tense motion rather than a lack of talent, and his hands-separate method rebuilds each hand's movement until it is relaxed.

    Practice plan
    Read Chapter 1, Sections 1 to 5 once, then apply it daily. Done when full relaxation on every chord release is automatic.

How the two halves connect hereThe idea is to decode away from the keys, then play relaxed. Dandelot and Westenra train your eyes off the keyboard, so that when you sit down to read the Kravchuk exercises, reading no longer fights the hands for attention, which is what keeps the wrists from locking. Chang's relaxation rule then governs how you press every one of those notes.

Practice rhythm

Daily · about 45 min

Five minutes of Dandelot note-naming, ten minutes of Westenra or musictheory.net intervals, fifteen minutes of Kravchuk on Sightread.dev, and fifteen relaxed minutes on a method-book page.

Weekly

Sight-read three brand-new Kravchuk exercises cold and note how many you play cleanly on the first try.

Monthly

Checkpoint

Checkpoint, am I ready for the next level?

≈ ABRSM / RCM Grade 1 (reading)

Confirm that fixed-position reading is automatic and tension-free before you start moving the hand around.

  1. Sight-read three random Kravchuk exercises hands-together, first attempt, with no tension.
  2. Speak a full grand-staff line at 60 BPM with no hesitation.
  3. Show complete hand relaxation on every chord release, which is Chang's own test.
  4. Confirm the pieces you are physically learning are about two to three grades above this reading material. Compare grades at PianoSyllabus.com.

When all four hold for a week, advance to Level 1.

Level 0 benchmark

How to use these benchmarks

A benchmark is a short, real piece, not an exercise. Its only job is to prove that the reading you built this level actually transfers to music before you move up. Do not drill it like repertoire.

  1. Listen to the recording once so you know the target sound.
  2. Sight-read the score cold, hands together, slow but steady — no stopping to fix notes.
  3. Pass: you get through it on the first or second attempt with a steady pulse and without watching your hands.
  4. Stall: if three honest attempts still fall apart, you are not ready. Don’t grind it — drop back, read easier material, then return. (This is the chart’s “New Game +”.)

The free Mayron Cole Audition Book page is the quick pass/fail gate. Passing this level lines up roughly with ABRSM / RCM Grade 1 reading.
