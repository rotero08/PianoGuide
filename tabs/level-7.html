Level 7: Polytonal Independence

Your goal for this level

Sight-read dense, non-traditional, and polyphonic scores of up to four voices hands-together, and run a self-sustaining practice loop that keeps improving you indefinitely.

There is no finish line. From here, improvement is simply a matter of reading and solving ever more diverse, dense material, for as long as you play.

How the two halves connect here

By this level reading and technique have merged. A fresh chorale is itself the technique session: you read four live voices hands-together, and the instant anything resists, every hands-separate tool you have built is already on hand. The loop sustains itself, which is what lifelong playing actually looks like.

Practice rhythm

Daily · 45 min or more

Fifteen minutes of counterpoint, fifteen minutes of Anki reviews, and fifteen minutes reading a fresh Bach chorale or polytonal passage cold.

Weekly

Choose a new higher-grade piece to fold into the loop.

Monthly

Audit broadly. The goal now is a wide range of repertoire, not a single grade.

Why there is no single test piece here

The source chart ends its graded benchmarks before Level 7: past this point reading is open-ended, so there is no one piece that certifies you. Instead, keep your own rolling benchmark — pull a fresh piece a grade above your comfort level and sight-read it cold, exactly as in earlier levels.

Use PianoSyllabus benchmarks to keep finding graded test pieces, and the Mayron Cole Audition Book for earlier-level self-checks.
