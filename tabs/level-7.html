Level 7 · the open road

Level 7: Polytonal Independence

Your goal for this level

Sight-read dense, non-traditional, and polyphonic scores of up to four voices hands-together, and run a self-sustaining practice loop that keeps improving you indefinitely.

There is no finish line. From here, improvement is simply a matter of reading and solving ever more diverse, dense material, for as long as you play.

Sight-Reading the eyes · hands-together

The densest common reading load: four-voice chorales and polytonal textures.

    Bach: 371 Harmonized ChoralesEndless four-voice sight-reading.

    A free collection of 371 four-voice chorales, an almost endless supply of dense reading. Reading a fresh one every day keeps your eye-hand span stretching, because four simultaneous voices is about the heaviest reading load you will meet.

    Practice plan
    Read a fresh chorale daily. Done when all four voices hold on a first read (this is ongoing).

How the two halves connect hereBy this level reading and technique have merged. A fresh chorale is itself the technique session: you read four live voices hands-together, and the instant anything resists, every hands-separate tool you have built is already on hand. The loop sustains itself, which is what lifelong playing actually looks like.

Practice rhythm

Daily · 45 min or more

Fifteen minutes of counterpoint, fifteen minutes of Anki reviews, and fifteen minutes reading a fresh Bach chorale or polytonal passage cold.

Weekly

Choose a new higher-grade piece to fold into the loop.

Monthly

Audit broadly. The goal now is a wide range of repertoire, not a single grade.

Checkpoint

Checkpoint, am I ready for the next level?

≈ ABRSM / RCM Grade 7 to 8 and beyond (reading)

There is no graduation, only a healthy, self-sustaining loop. Confirm it is running.

  1. Sight-read an unseen four-voice chorale hands-together with all voices intact.
  2. Keep the reading-versus-playing gap honest, with reading material two to three grades under your hardest pieces.
  3. Maintain a clear Anki deck and reach for the right hands-separate tool automatically.
  4. Keep widening your repertoire. Compare grades and pick new challenges at PianoSyllabus.com.

Keep the loop running. This is what lifelong playing looks like.
