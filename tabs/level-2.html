Level 2 · two independent voices

Level 2: Voices & Keyboard Geometry

Your goal for this level

Read and play two independent melodic lines at the same time, read key signatures as keyboard terrain rather than as arithmetic, and navigate scales and triads as an automatic map under the hand.

Real polyphony begins, meaning both hands play distinct lines instead of melody plus accompaniment. That demands a genuine internal map of the keyboard.

Sight-Reading the eyes · hands-together

Two-voice reading, plus your first key signatures and accidentals.

    Kunz: 200 Short Two-Part CanonsThe classic two-voice independence trainer.

    A free collection written precisely to train hand independence, where the left hand imitates the right a beat later. Because the two parts are equal and interlocking, these canons are the standard way to break the habit of the left hand passively following the right.

    Practice plan
    Read canons 1 to 30, a couple a day. Done when both voices stay even hands-together.

Technique the hands · hands-separate

Add fluency with key signatures, and drill the geometry your hands navigate by feel so your eyes can stay on the page.

    Westenra: Read Music FAST (Part 2)Ledger lines, key signatures, accidentals.

    The second half of the landmark-reading course, now adding ledger lines, key signatures, and accidentals. It presents sharps and flats as changes to the physical landscape of the keyboard rather than rules to compute.

    Practice plan
    Ten minutes daily. Done when you read three ledger lines and any key signature on sight.
    Open resource ↗
    Keyboard geometry: scales & triadsScales and triads, the spatial map.

    The home for scale and triad practice, which builds the spatial map both hands rely on. Drilling the day's key is what lets your left hand hold an independent line without your eyes leaving the page.

    Practice plan
    Ten minutes of the day's key daily. Done when you run it evenly with your eyes up.
    Notation theoryLearn key signatures away from the keys.

    Learn how key signatures and accidentals actually work on paper, away from the keys, in the How To Read Music tab. Understanding them in the abstract first stops them from stealing attention later when you are reading at speed.

    Practice plan
    Study before drilling at speed. Done when key signatures need no conscious thought.
How the two halves connect hereIndependent voices need a map that the page does not give you. Learning key signatures away from the keys, plus daily scale geometry, means your left hand can hold its own line by touch while your eyes track the right. The old advice to stop staring at your hands only becomes possible once that geometry is automatic.

Checkpoint

Checkpoint, am I ready for the next level?

≈ ABRSM / RCM Grade 2 (reading)

Confirm genuine two-hand independence while reading key signatures.

  1. Read a new two-voice piece hands-together, keeping both lines even.
  2. Play scales and triads of the week's key smoothly and tension-free with your eyes up.
  3. Read ledger notes up to three lines beyond each staff without hesitation.
  4. Confirm your reading material is still two to three grades under your playing level. Check grades here.

When all four hold, move to Level 3.
