Pre-Staff: Arm Weight & the Ear

Your goal for this level

Sit relaxed, find any key by feel without looking down, match a played pitch with your voice, and play simple five-finger patterns hands-together from directional (arrow) notation.

Before the staff appears, you build the two things everything else rests on: a relaxed body and a working ear. Reading at this stage is purely directional, just arrows showing up or down, so all of your attention can go to relaxation and keyboard geometry.

Sight-Reading the eyes · hands-together

Graphic and arrow notation only. The eyes simply learn that higher means move right and lower means move left.

    Mayron Cole: Pre-Level 1 Free Recommended Arrow-and-finger-number method to build coordination before the staff.

    Uses directional arrows and simple finger indexes instead of a staff. This removes decoding friction, focusing attention entirely on developing hand alignment and visual spacing.

    Practice plan
    Play one new page a day. Done when you play five arrow patterns without looking down.
