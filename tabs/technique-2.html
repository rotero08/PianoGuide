Technique 2: Hand Shapes & Hands-Separate Control

Your goal for this level

Each hand, on its own, can play even five-finger patterns and find a basic triad shape by feel, staying relaxed throughout. Still no sight-reading—you are training the hands so that when reading begins, the body is never the bottleneck.

With a calm body in place from Technique 1, the second foundation is control of each hand separately. Building each hand alone, the way Chang teaches, is faster and cleaner than fighting both at once, and sets up the hands-together reading that the Sight-Reading Curriculum begins next.

Technique only finger control · hand geometry

Even five-finger work, gentle daily warm-ups, and the feel of a triad under the hand. Everything here is played hands separately and from feel, not from a printed score.

These same triad shapes return the moment you reach lead sheets. Building them by feel now means that later you read a chord symbol and your hand already knows the shape.

Practice rhythm

Daily · about 15 min

One warm-up group and one five-finger pattern per hand, then shape two or three triads, always slow and relaxed.

Weekly

Check that the weaker hand is keeping up with the stronger one; give it the extra reps.

Checkpoint, am I ready to start sight-reading?

Technique foundation · no grade yet

Passing this is the moment the Sight-Reading Curriculum begins, at Pre-Staff.

  1. Each hand alone plays an even five-finger pattern, relaxed throughout.
  2. You complete a daily warm-up group per hand with no strain.
  3. You can land a basic triad as a single shape, by feel.
  4. Nothing hurts, and you can still release tension on every release.

When all four hold, go to Pre-Staff and begin reading.
