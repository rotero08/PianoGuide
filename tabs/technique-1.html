Technique 1: Body, Bench & Arm Weight

Your goal for this level

Sit relaxed at the correct height with level wrists, and learn to make sound by dropping a loose arm into the key instead of pressing with the fingers. No notation or sight-reading yet—just a calm, tension-free body.

This guide puts technique before sight-reading. Before a single note is read, you build the relaxed physical habit that everything else depends on. Almost every self-taught stall traces back to tension that set in early, so this is where you start.

Posture, Biomechanics & Arm Weight

Posture, bench height, and the arm-weight drop. Watch each demonstration once, then practise the motion away from any printed music.

    Alan Belkin: Arm Weight & PostureVideoRecommendedThe single most important first habit.

    Why this is recommended: A conservatory professor demonstrates letting a relaxed arm fall into the key rather than pressing with the fingers. Copying this loose, springy wrist drop prevents the stiff, finger-driven habit that causes most early tension.

    Practice plan
    Watch once, then drop each hand daily. Done when your wrist stays loose and springy on every note.
