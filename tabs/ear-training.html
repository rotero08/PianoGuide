Playing by Ear & Audiation

Playing by ear is often described less as an inherited gift and more as a trainable skill. The consensus among ear-training teachers is that practising it strengthens relative pitch and audiation.

By learning to identify intervals, resolve tonal centers, and play simple melodies by ear, you build a strong mental connection between your hands and the sounds you hear, which directly supports your sight-reading fluency.
