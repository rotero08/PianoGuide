Level 3: Positional Shifting

Your goal for this level

Read and play music that moves the hand out of a fixed position, with leaps, shifts, and broken patterns, while keeping your eyes on the page and your wrist loose.

Stationary reading ends here. Pieces now travel across the keyboard, so you learn to find new positions by touch instead of looking down.

How to use these benchmarks & calibrate difficulty

A benchmark is a short, real piece, not an exercise. Its only job is to prove that the reading you built at this level actually transfers to music before you move up. Do not drill it like repertoire.

  1. Listen to the recording once so you know the target sound.
  2. Sight-read the score cold, hands together, slow but steady — no stopping to fix notes.
  3. Pass: You get through it on the first or second attempt with a steady pulse and without watching your hands.
  4. Stall: If three honest attempts still fall apart, you are not ready. Don't grind it — drop back, read easier material, then return. (This is the chart’s "New Game +".)

Measuring your reading level via PianoSyllabus: Go to the PianoSyllabus search form. Enter a piece you currently play comfortably with correct phrasing. Check its normalized grade. If it is listed as RCM/ABRSM Grade 4, that represents your current playing ceiling. Subtract 2 or 3 grades to find your sight-reading band (Grade 2). Pull three unseen pieces from Grade 2 and attempt to sight-read them. If you read them with a steady pulse on the first pass, your coordinates are fully aligned [1.4].

The Era Repertoire Rule: You only need to successfully sight-read one of the era benchmark pieces to clear a level and advance. The five historical eras (Baroque, Classical, Romantic, C20, and Modern) are provided as options to give you flexibility. If one piece isn't clicking, try another! However, returning to these benchmarks later and passing all five is recommended to prove your reading is general and versatile rather than pattern-matched to a single composer.

The five era pieces (Baroque through Modern) test the same reading across different musical styles, so passing all five means your reading is general rather than pattern-matched to one composer. The free Mayron Cole Audition Book page is the quick pass/fail gate. Passing this level lines up roughly with around ABRSM / RCM Grade 3.
