Why this exists

Self-taught players rarely stall for lack of effort; they stall because their tips never form a sequence. This guide collects what working teachers and the r/piano community recommend most, and arranges it into one path you can actually follow in order.

How it's built

Relaxed technique leads, and sight-reading joins once your hands are calm, with the two then growing side by side, level by level, all the way to fluent reading of real repertoire. Ear, harmony, and theory feed in exactly where they help most.
