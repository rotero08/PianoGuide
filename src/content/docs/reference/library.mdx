Every claim in this guide about how the brain reads, how the hands move, and how skills consolidate traces back to published research. The full bibliography is collected here, followed by an auxiliary library of theory, pedagogy, and acoustics resources you can search and filter.

## Scientific Bibliography

<Bibliography>
  * #ref-1 **Rubin-Rabson, G. (1940).** *Studies in the psychology of memorizing piano music.* Journal of Educational Psychology. Early evidence that spaced, distributed practice beats massed repetition for keyboard retention.
  * #ref-2 **Schmidt, R. A., &amp; Lee, T. D. (2011).** *Motor Control and Learning: A Behavioral Emphasis (5th ed.).* Human Kinetics. Distributed practice and the decoupling of the two hands during skill acquisition.
  * #ref-3 **Gordon, E. E. (1975).** *Learning Sequences in Music.* GIA Publications. Defines audiation, the inner hearing of music.
  * #ref-4 **Brodsky, W., et al. (2008).** *The Mental Representation of Music Notation: Notational Audiation.* JEP: HPP, 34(2), 427-445. Silent reading of notation triggers measurable vocal subvocalization.
  * #ref-5 **Sloboda, J. A. (1984).** *The Musical Mind.* Oxford University Press. Foundational work on gaze tracking and visual chunking in reading.
  * #ref-6 **Sweller, J. (1988).** *Cognitive Load Theory.* Journal of Educational Psychology, 80(2), 162-185. The four to seven item working-memory limit and the split-attention effect.
  * #ref-7 **Walker, M. P., Brakefield, T., Hobson, J. A., &amp; Stickgold, R. (2002).** *Sleep-dependent learning and motor cortical plasticity.* Neuron, 34(1), 141-145. Motor gains consolidate during sleep, not the practice session.
  * #ref-8 **Ericsson, K. A., Krampe, R. T., &amp; Tesch-Romer, C. (1993).** *The role of deliberate practice in the acquisition of expert performance.* Psychological Review, 100(3), 363-406.
  * #ref-9 **Goebl, W., &amp; Palmer, C. (2008).** *Tactile feedback in piano performance.* Journal of the Acoustical Society of America, 124(1), 1-11. Saccades and feedforward eye-hand coordination.
  * #ref-10 **Highben, Z., &amp; Palmer, C. (2004).** *Effects of Auditory and Motor Mental Practice in Memorized Piano Performance.* Bulletin of CRME, 159, 58-65. Aural skill predicts accuracy when feedback is removed.
  * #ref-11 **Furuya, S., &amp; Altenmuller, E. (2013).** *Flexibility of motor organization in pianists.* Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, 7, 201. Co-contraction and antagonistic muscle firing.
  * #ref-12 **Finney, S. A. (1997).** *Auditory feedback in piano performance.* Journal of Experimental Psychology: HPP, 23(1), 242-259.
  * #ref-13 **Pfordresher, P. Q. (2003).** *Sensory-motor coordination in music performance.* Acta Psychologica, 113(3), 269-291.
  * #ref-14 **Goolsby, T. W. (1994).** *Eye Movement in Music Reading.* Music Perception, 12(1), 77-96.
  * #ref-15 **Furneaux, S., &amp; Land, M. F. (1999).** *The effects of skill on the eye-hand span during musical sight-reading.* Proc. R. Soc. Lond. B, 266(1436), 2435-2440.
  * #ref-16 **Waters, A., Townsend, E., &amp; Underwood, G. (1998).** *Expertise in musical sight reading.* British Journal of Psychology, 89(1), 123-149.
  * #ref-17 **Drake, C., &amp; Palmer, C. (2000).** *Skill acquisition in music performance.* Psychology of Music, 28(2), 150-168.
  * #ref-18 **Kopiez, R., &amp; Lee, J. I. (2008).** *Towards a general model of skills involved in sight reading music.* Music Education Research, 10(1), 41-62.
  * #ref-19 **Wristen, B. (2005).** *Cognition and Motor Execution in Piano Sight-Reading.* Cognitive Science, 29(4), 112-131.
</Bibliography>
