The top of the YouTube piano space is genuinely excellent, but the algorithm rewards engagement rather than pedagogy, so a Juilliard graduate and a self-taught chord player can both have 500k subscribers. Below, sorted by what each channel actually does best. Full credit to every creator.

<ScreenTrap>
  Screens split your attention. Following Anders Ericsson's *Peak* principles, real deliberate practice happens with a **printed, physical score on the music stand and every electronic device kept at a distance**. Constantly returning to a phone or tablet to pause, rewind, and squint at hand positions turns the device into a distraction rather than a tool. Use YouTube for what it is uniquely good at, namely **off-keyboard visual demonstration, discovery, and inspiration**, and then close it before you practise. You will learn a piece far faster with a printed sheet and nothing else in front of you.
</ScreenTrap>

## Classical depth & technique

<Grid>
  <Card title="Nahre Sol" subs="~826K" focus="Classical · composition" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Nahre+Sol">
    Juilliard-trained, Artist Diploma from the Glenn Gould School, first "Creator in Residence" at Hamburg's Elbphilharmonie. Bridges classical technique and modern composition; her "13 Levels of Piano Technique" is a platform landmark.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dr. Josh Wright" subs="~200K" focus="Classical technique" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Dr+Josh+Wright+piano">
    DMA (University of Michigan) and Billboard No. 1 classical artist. 700+ videos with dual camera angles, invaluable for seeing exactly how the hands move. ProPractice Academy extends the free content.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Paul Barton" subs="~700K" focus="Classical · performance" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Paul+Barton+piano">
    2,000+ classical tutorials and full performances from his Bangkok studio, and famous for playing Beethoven to rescue elephants in Thailand, which tells you about the gentleness of his teaching.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mangold Project" subs="~790K" focus="Classical simplifications" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Mangold+Project">
    Approachable classical simplifications and arrangements that make iconic repertoire playable earlier.
  </Card>
</Grid>

## Adult learning & method

<Grid>
  <Card title="Pianote" subs="~2.39M" focus="Method · pop/rock/blues" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Pianote">
    The largest piano education channel and a funnel into Musora Media's paid platform. Instructors Lisa Witt and Colin Crump deliver a ten-level "Method" with 500+ song tutorials; the free content alone is substantial.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Zach Evans" subs="~913K" focus="Accelerated · adult beginners" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Become+a+Piano+Superhuman+Zach+Evans">
    "Become a Piano Superhuman." Accelerated learning strategies, practice psychology, and beginner-friendly chord progressions, explicitly for adults who want fast, visible progress.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Jazer Lee" subs="~755K" focus="Habits · the mental game" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Jazer+Lee+piano">
    Focuses on the psychology of adult learning: habit correction, technique streamlining, and fitting practice around a busy work life.
  </Card>
  <Card title="PianoTV (Allysia Van Betuw)" subs="~148K" focus="Beginner theory + piano" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=PianoTV+Allysia+Van+Betuw">
    Beginner-friendly theory integrated directly with piano learning, a gentle on-ramp for self-taught players.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Andrew Furmanczyk" subs="~527K" focus="Free course pioneer" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Andrew+Furmanczyk+piano">
    A pioneer of free YouTube piano education since 2006, with a complete beginner course structure.
  </Card>
</Grid>

## Chords, jazz & improvisation

<Grid>
  <Card title="Bill Hilton" subs="~429K" focus="Chord-based · cocktail jazz" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Bill+Hilton+piano">
    One of the longest-running channels (since 2008). The go-to for chord-based playing, cocktail jazz, and improvisation; his book "How to Really Play the Piano" prizes intuition over rote learning. Best starting point for playing from a chord chart.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Julian Bradley" subs="~418K" focus="Jazz theory · voicings" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Julian+Bradley+Jazz+Tutorial">
    Master's in composition. The most comprehensive free jazz piano education on YouTube, covering voicings, improvisation, and jazz scales in a structured, step-by-step approach.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Aimee Nolte" subs="~300K+" focus="Jazz · vocal/scat" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Aimee+Nolte+Music">
    Uniquely combines jazz piano with vocal and scat education; her ii–V–I tutorials and standard analyses draw on real performance experience.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Charles Cornell" subs="~1.4M" focus="Jazz · reharmonisation" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Charles+Cornell">
    Purchase College jazz grad who blends comedy with real music education. His "5 Versions" series makes reharmonisation and chord substitution surprisingly accessible.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Adam Neely" subs="~1.81M" focus="Deep theory essays" link="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Adam+Neely">
    Long-form music theory essays that go far beyond the keyboard, for when you want the "why" behind the harmony.
  </Card>
</Grid>

<Callout title="How to use this list" bordered={true} bgColor="tint">
  Pick one classical and one chord/jazz channel that match your goals, watch for demonstration and inspiration, then return to your printed scores and the levels above. Discovery on screen, practice on paper.
</Callout>
