*Level 4 · expression and speed*

<Callout bordered={true} accentColor="gold" label="Your goal for this level" icon="target">
  Read dynamics, articulation, ledger lines, and changing tempos on a first pass, read chord inversions by feel, and handle passages too fast for plain repetition along with your first three-against-four polyrhythm.
</Callout>

Reading becomes expressive and dense. Technique gains two specific tools for passages that resist the fingers, and you meet your first independent-rhythm coordination challenge.

  <BenchmarkGuide title="How to use these benchmarks & calibrate difficulty">
    A benchmark is a short, real piece, not an exercise. Its only job is to prove that the reading you built at this level actually transfers to music before you move up. Do not drill it like repertoire.

    1. **Listen** to the recording once so you know the target sound.
    2. **Sight-read** the score cold, hands together, slow but steady — no stopping to fix notes.
    3. **Pass:** You get through it on the first or second attempt with a steady pulse and without watching your hands.
    4. **Stall:** If three honest attempts still fall apart, you are not ready. Don't grind it — drop back, read easier material, then return. (This is the chart's "New Game +".)

    **Measuring your reading level via PianoSyllabus:** Go to the [PianoSyllabus search form](https://pianosyllabus.com). Enter a piece you currently play comfortably with correct phrasing. Check its normalized grade. If it is listed as RCM/ABRSM Grade 6, that represents your current *playing ceiling*. Subtract 2 or 3 grades to find your *sight-reading band* (Grade 3–4). Pull three unseen pieces from Grade 3 or 4 and attempt to sight-read them. If you read them with a steady pulse on the first pass, your coordinates are fully aligned [1.4].

    **The Era Repertoire Rule:** You only need to successfully sight-read **one** of the era benchmark pieces to clear a level and advance. The five historical eras (Baroque, Classical, Romantic, C20, and Modern) are provided as options to give you flexibility. If one piece isn't clicking, try another! However, returning to these benchmarks later and passing **all five** is recommended to prove your reading is general and versatile rather than pattern-matched to a single composer.

    The five era pieces test the same reading across different musical styles. The free Mayron Cole Audition Book page is the quick pass/fail gate. Passing this level lines up roughly with ABRSM / RCM Grade 3–4.
  </BenchmarkGuide>
