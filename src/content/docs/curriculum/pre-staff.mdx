Before the staff appears, you build the two things everything else rests on: a relaxed body and a working ear. Reading at this stage is purely directional, just arrows showing up or down, so all of your attention can go to relaxation and keyboard geometry.

<Callout bordered={true} accentColor="gold" label="Your goal for this level">
  Sit relaxed, find any key by feel without looking down, match a played pitch with your voice, and play simple five-finger patterns hands-together from directional (arrow) notation.
</Callout>

This guide puts **technique before sight-reading**. Before a single note is read, you build the relaxed physical habit that everything else depends on. Almost every self-taught stall traces back to tension that set in early, so this is where you start.

## Level Resources

<Grid>
  <Resource title="Mayron Cole: Pre-Level 1" link="https://www.freepianomethod.com/pre-level-1.html" tags="free recommended" icon="star">
    Uses directional arrows and simple finger indexes instead of a staff. This removes decoding friction, focusing attention entirely on developing hand alignment and visual spacing.
    
    **Practice plan:** Play one new page a day. Done when you play five arrow patterns without looking down.
  </Resource>
  
  <Resource title="William Curtis: First Steps to Ear Training" link="https://www.amazon.com/First-Steps-Ear-Training-Curtis/dp/0876390165" tags="paid" icon="target">
    Vocal pitch-matching exercises to build cognitive audiation. Sing back the exact pitch a key plays to verify that your brain can hold and predict pitch accurately.
    
    **Practice plan:** Pitch match 10 random keys daily. Done when your pitch matching holds steadily.
  </Resource>
</Grid>
