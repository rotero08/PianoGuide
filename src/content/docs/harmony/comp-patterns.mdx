Once basic chord shapes are reliable, a frequent next step suggested by accompanists is to move beyond plain blocked chords. Comping patterns, or "vamps", are widely used to create more dynamic, rhythmic accompaniment.

By breaking up triads into patterns, arpeggios, and syncopated rhythms, you can easily accompany vocalists or improvise in different musical styles.
